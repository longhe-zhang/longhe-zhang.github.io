逆向记忆中的记忆载体——对南京大屠杀死难者国家公祭报道（2014～2023）的研究

Memory Carriers in Reversed Memory: A Study of Reports for the National Memorial Day for Nanjing Massacre Victims (2014-2023).

黄顺铭 & 张龙赫. (2025). 新闻记者, (10), 15-34. CSSCI 来源期刊.

Abstract / 摘要

作为一种由新闻媒体建构的集体记忆形式，逆向记忆是一种通过叙述当下事件以纪念过往事件的叙事， 往往藉由“人”、“地”、“物”和“象”四种基本记忆载体来展开叙事，以实现“过去的持续”。 本文从“逆向记忆”的理论视角出发，结合内容分析法、社会网络分析法和个案研究法，从“属性”、 “关系”和“文本”的层面，层层递进地勾勒了我国自设立国家公祭日以来最初十年间（2014～2023） 的国家公祭逆向记忆报道中的记忆载体面貌。本文最后还提出未来的研究可致力于构建一个逆向记忆的 基本修辞框架，以进一步深化对此概念的理解。本文有助于我们更好地认识新闻媒体在建构南京大屠杀 集体记忆过程中的作用机制。

As a form of collective memory constructed by news media, reversed memory refers to narratives that commemorate past events by reporting and narrating present events. Such narratives often unfold through four basic memory carriers: persons, places, objects, and images, thereby sustaining the presence of the past. From the theoretical perspective of reversed memory, this article combines content analysis, social network analysis, and case study methods to map the memory carriers in the first decade (2014-2023) of Chinese news coverage of the National Memorial Ceremony. It examines these carriers through the dimensions of attributes, relations, and texts. The article further suggests that future research may build a rhetorical framework of reversed memory to deepen understanding of the concept and to better explain how news media construct the collective memory of the Nanjing Massacre.

From Social Spaces to Distributed Memory Networks: Technological Maneuvering and Digital Memory in WeChat User Space Management Practices

Audience section.

Abstract / 摘要

自连接转向以降，讨论数字记忆与联结记忆的研究层出不穷。以往研究偏向以“连接”的视角切入， 讨论“诸众记忆”的建构或数字记忆的特性等主题。与之不同，本文将微信空间管理视作人们自下而上地 形塑个体数字记忆的过程，通过深度访谈、走查法和文本分析的质性研究方法，聚焦微信用户的空间管理 动机、技术扰动、管理策略以及其中形塑的数字记忆图景。研究发现，源于系统与平台的“存储压迫”构成 用户空间管理实践的被迫起点，并使得用户卷入多重界面的循环博弈之中。而通过清理、备份与设备置换等 操作，用户逐渐形成以“防御性存档”为核心的归档策略，进而将碎片化、分布式存储的“记忆数据”整合为 介于个体与集体之间、私人与公众之间的“诸众记忆档案”。这一档案呈现出跨时空的延展特性和脆弱的记忆 韧性，微信也从传统的社交平台，转变为一个去中心化的、高度个人化的分布式记忆网络。本文认为“微信用户 空间管理实践”是人们在数字技术中介下形成的“新型记忆范式”。

Since the connective turn, studies of digital memory and connective memory have grown rapidly. Previous research has tended to approach these questions through the lens of connection, examining the construction of multitude memory or the characteristics of digital memory. In contrast, this article treats WeChat space management as a bottom-up process through which users shape individual digital memory. Drawing on in-depth interviews, walkthrough methods, and textual analysis, it examines users' motivations, technological disturbances, management strategies, and the digital memory landscape shaped through these practices. The study finds that storage pressure generated by systems and platforms becomes the forced starting point of users' space management practices, drawing them into cyclical negotiations across multiple interfaces. Through deletion, backup, and device replacement, users gradually develop archival strategies centered on defensive archiving, integrating fragmented and distributed memory data into multitude memory archives that sit between the individual and the collective, the private and the public. These archives show trans-spatial and trans-temporal extension as well as fragile memory resilience. WeChat thus shifts from a conventional social platform into a decentralized and highly personalized distributed memory network. The article argues that WeChat user space management practices constitute a new memory paradigm mediated by digital technology.
